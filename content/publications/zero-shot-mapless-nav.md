Enabling mobile robots to navigate unknown environments from natural language instructions is a key challenge in embodied AI. We propose DualNav, a zero-shot mapless navigation framework that decouples high-frequency reactive control from low-frequency semantic reasoning via an asynchronous parallel architecture. DualNav compensates for foundation model inference latency and maintains spatio-temporal consistency through timestamp-based state synchronization. An active visual-semantic memory and orientation-constrained local planner further enhance robustness and perceptual persistence. Experiments in simulation and real-world environments show DualNav achieves significant improvements in navigation success rate and motion smoothness over serial baselines.
