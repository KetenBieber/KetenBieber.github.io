Enabling mobile robots to navigate unknown environments from natural language instructions is crucial for embodied intelligence. We propose a zero-shot mapless navigation framework with a dual-rate asynchronous architecture, decoupling high-frequency control from low-frequency semantic reasoning. Our method uses timestamp-driven state synchronization and an active visual-semantic memory for robust target tracking, and incorporates visibility-aware constraints for continuous target observation. Experiments in simulation and real-world settings show that DualNav significantly improves navigation success rate, motion continuity, and perceptual persistence over baseline methods.
